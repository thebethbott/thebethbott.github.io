The primary issue areas at the forefront of
the Court's cumulative caseload include:

  1. Economic activity- wage arbitration, property claims, and corruption
  2. Criminal procedure- cruel and unusual punishment, right to council, and illegal search and seizure
  3. Judicial power- jurisdiction, certiorari and mootness


Analysis

Rationally property and safety are at the fundamental core of any rule of law society, and Judicial power is the organizing principal by which both are determined— logically these are the primary areas of focus. Far fewer cases fundamentally change our rights and protections in areas elsewhere in our lives such as our ability to speak without judicial consequence and our ability to organize labor movements.

However, when we look to determine if a primary function of the Court has been to pursue activist social movements, we cannot deny that a primary function of the Court is also Civil Rights which range broadly from affirmative action and integration to voting rights and jurisdiction limitations over indigenous peoples.

While the Court remains true to its function in the constitution, simply as enforcement over policy determine by the legislature, by pushing the boundaries of the interpretations of the constitution, it also has in fact exercised its power to adjudicate beyond the scope of existing agreed upon laws.
